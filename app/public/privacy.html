Privacy Policy.

EFFECTIVE 12 JULY 2026 · CINEFOLIO.DEV

This policy explains what CineFolio Studios collects, why, where it lives, and the rights you keep. The data controller is Mohammed Ait El Qadi, operating CineFolio Studios from Budapest, Hungary. Reach us any time at info@cinefolio.dev.

01What we collect, and why

DataWhy we have it

We do not run third-party advertising trackers, we do not sell data, and we do not build visitor profiles of the people who watch your film.

02Your photos and AI production

When you order an AI film, the photos you upload are used solely as identity references to generate stylized scenes of you. For that generation step, your photos and brief are shared with our AI production provider strictly to render your order, and for no other purpose. We never use your photos to train models, to generate anyone else's likeness, or for marketing without your explicit permission.

03Where your data lives

CineFolio runs on Amazon Web Services in the EU (Frankfurt, eu-central-1): storage, database, and hosting live there, with encryption at rest for private materials. Transactional email is sent via AWS SES. Some processors (payments, AI generation) may process data outside the EU under their standard contractual safeguards.

04Processors we use

  • Amazon Web Services — infrastructure, storage, and email delivery
  • Lemon Squeezy (Sold through Link, LLC) — merchant of record for paid purchases: they process payment and billing data as the seller; card details never reach CineFolio
  • AI generation provider — renders the film scenes for orders you place, from the materials you provided

05Cookies and local storage

The studio console uses local storage and session tokens strictly to keep you signed in and remember your workspace state. The marketing site sets no advertising cookies. Premiered films carry a first-party view beacon that increments a daily counter — no cookies, no fingerprinting, no cross-site anything.

06Retention and deletion

Your content stays as long as your account and films do. Unpublish removes a film from the public web immediately; hard delete permanently removes its releases, records, and stored files and frees your subdomain. Deleting your account removes your profile and content, except minimal records we must keep for legal or accounting reasons (order history tied to payments). Deletion is real deletion, not a soft flag.

07Your rights (GDPR)

You can request access to, correction of, export of, or erasure of your personal data, and you can object to or restrict processing. Most of it you can do yourself in the console; for the rest, email info@cinefolio.dev and we will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority — in Hungary, the NAIH (naih.hu).

08Legal bases

We process your data to perform our contract with you (producing and hosting your films), on your consent where we ask for it (e.g., featuring your premiere in the showcase), and on legitimate interest for keeping the platform secure and improving it — never in ways that override your rights.

09Changes

If this policy changes materially, we update this page with a new effective date and notify account holders by email. Silence is never treated as consent for new uses of your data.
